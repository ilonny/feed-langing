Other data
We may process other data we collect and process in relation with the App, for example data related to complaints and to investigations of alleged misconduct etc.

7. How we use cookies and similar technologies

We use cookies and 3rd party beacons on our website (currently https://feedapp.me) and in the App. We use these technologies in order to ensure and improve the quality of the App and website. We do not use these technologies for retargeting except for a short period of not more than one month. When you use the website or App, you consent to us using these technologies.

The App and our website use Google Analytics, a web analytics service provided by Google Ireland Ltd. in Ireland or Google LLC in the USA (“Google”). Google analyses your use of our website and compiles reports on your website activity on this basis to helps us optimise our website. Google uses cookies, which store information collected by Google about your behaviour on our website. This information can later be read by Google. This information will be transmitted to and stored by Google on servers in the United States. We have configured Google Analytics such that your IP address is truncated by Google before transmission to the USA. Only in exceptional cases will the full IP address be transmitted to a Google server in the USA and shortened there. Google does not provide us with any information about your identity. Google may collect information about your use of our website, combine this information with data from other websites that you have visited, and which also work with Google, and use this information for its own purposes (e.g. to control advertising). If you have an account with Google, Google may also identify you. Google is responsible for processing your personal data in accordance with Google's privacy policy.

You can prevent the installation of cookies by choosing the appropriate browser settings. However, in this case you may not be able to use all the full functionality of our website. You can also download and install an opt-out browser plug-in: Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on.

We also use Google Firebase in our App in order to collect information about your use of the App, information about the device on which you install the App, including your country/location, the device make and model, its operating system, when the App was opened your language settings, the Identifier for Advertising (iOS) and the Google Advertising ID (for Android), in order to understand your behaviour better and optimize our App. We use such information in order to understand the use of our App and identify where the performance of the App can be improved to address performance issues. Firebase (Google) also remembers settings of the mobile app (like appearance, A/B test settings, custom content or updates), and evaluates and reacts to processes within the App. In A/B tests, individual functions are introduced on a test basis before the release of a new version of the App, and the effects of the adjustments during use can be analysed. Firebase is also used to track and fix app stability issues (technical errors and crashes) in real time. In the event of a crash, anonymous information about the device used and the use of the App is transmitted to Google’s servers in the USA to determine the cause. Crash reports do not contain personal data. You can object to data processing by Crashlytics at any time by deactivating the “Send crash reports” function in the App settings.For more information on Firebase please visit Privacy and Security in Firebase, or Firebase Data Processing and Security Terms, or Crashlytics and App Distribution Data Processing and Security Terms.

We also use Amplitude in our App and website, an analysis service of Amplitude Inc. (631 Howard Street, Suite 300, San Francisco, CA 94105, USA). Amplitude collects technical information, such as the type of your device and its operating system. In addition, Amplitude tracks location data (e.g. country) and internal data (e.g. language, server upload time, session ID) that occur when using our website. Such events may, for example, be how long you were logged into our website and from which country. You will find additional information about the manner in which Amplitude processes data at https://amplitude.com/privacy.

8. Your rights

I relation with the processing of your personal data, you have the following rights:

Access right
You have the right to request disclosure of the personal data we store and process. If you have any questions regarding processing you may at any time contact us at the contact details in Section 1.
Rectification right
You have the right to request that we rectify incorrectly stored data. You also are entitled to have incomplete personal data completed.
Erasure right
You have the right to ask us to erase your personal data. You may exercise this right, for example, if you consider that the personal data is no longer necessary for the purpose for which it was collected or if you withdraw your consent to processing. Note that we may not comply with an erasure request as authorized by applicable law.
Right to restriction of processing
You have the right to request us to restrict processing operations on personal data, for example, if we no longer need the personal data but you require it for legal purposes.
Right to have Personal Data transferred
You have the right to receive certain personal data in a structured, commonly used and machine-readable format and to request that we transfer the data to another controller. The right to transfer personal data to another controller is only available if this is technically possible.
Right to lodge a complaint with the supervisory authority
If you intend to lodge a complaint about our processing of your personal data, you may at any time contact the relevant local supervisory authority or lodge a formal complaint directly, which may be found at https://edpb.europa.eu/about-edpb/board/members_en where the GDPR applies.
Right to withdraw consent
If processing of your personal data is based on your consent, you have the right to withdraw your consent at any time. Your withdrawal will not affect the lawfulness of the processing carried out before you withdrew your consent.

If you wish to exercise any rights described above or want to obtain more information about it, please contact us as set out in Section 1. Please note that your rights set out above may be subject to conditions or restrictions under applicable data protection laws.

We may need to request information from you to help us confirm your identity and ensure your right to access your personal data or to exercise any of your other rights. This is a security measure to ensure that personal data is not disclosed to any other person who has no right to receive it. We may also contact you to ask you for further information in relation to your request to speed up our response.
